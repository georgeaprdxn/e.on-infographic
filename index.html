why companies are going electric

And they’re not the only ones: the UK government wants half of all new cars to be ultra-low emission by 2030.

The number of electric vehicles (EVs) and charging points in Britain is increasing exponentially, with clear evidence that government, businesses and consumers are taking this clean form of transportation seriously.

government action: carrots and sticks

The UK government is banning sales of new conventional diesel and petrol cars and vans from 2040, and providing a £400m fund to help roll out charging infrastructure. The government is also supporting individuals and businesses in the transition, offering:

  • plug-in hybrid cards

  • pure electric vehicles

  • electric vans

  • installing charge points to a business
